So I am completely obsessed with no-wave. A while back, I started getting into some [Factory Records](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Factory_Records) goodness ([A Certain Ratio](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Certain_Ratio), [Durutti Column](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durutti_column), etc) and my friend from [East Hill CD](http://www.easthillcd.com/) told me about [James Chance and the Contortions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chance_%26_the_Contortions) At first, I thought James’ sax playing was too chaotic and abrasive, but thanks to the just recently purchased [Live in New York](http://musicbrainz.org/album/49754756-5c7b-4008-88e3-7e29af0ac702.html) album, it really sold me on how innovative they were back in the 80’s. It also made me crave James’ singing. It’s toxic! And to think…

> *“No Wave was a short-lived but influential offshoot of punk rock centered in New York City during the late 1970s and early 1980s. The term No Wave was partly a satiric wordplay rejecting the commercial elements of the then-popular New Wave genre, and also a declaration of the music’s experimental nature: No Wave music belonged to no fixed style or genre.”*

Now, this has lead me onto many other wonderful artists such as: [Teenage Jesus & the Jerks](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teenage_Jesus_%26_the_Jerks), [Lydia Lunch](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lydia_Lunch), [Mars](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars_%28band%29), and thanks to a guy at [Vinyl Fever](http://www.vinylfever.com/), [DNA](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_%28band%29), which I’m not fully into yet. Teenage Jesus & the Jerks are quite amazing.
